# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary users are couples and small family-like groups who want lightweight visibility into each other's fasting activity. They may also use the app alone for Personal Fasting Activity before joining a Fasting Circle.

Users are likely checking the app during ordinary daily moments: before meals, during work, in the evening, or when casually checking on a partner's current Fasting Status. The interface should feel easy to return to, private by default, and supportive without becoming noisy.

## Product Purpose

The app helps users start, track, end, and correct Fasting Sessions, while optionally sharing current fasting activity with trusted Circle Members.

Success means a user can understand their own current Fasting Status at a glance, feel confident about what is visible to others, and support a partner or small circle without the app feeling like a social feed, competition, or health dashboard.

## Brand Personality

Calm, private, supportive, warm, intimate.

The visual and interaction language should feel aesthetic and calming: soft enough for repeated personal use, polished enough to feel cared for, and warm enough to feel welcoming rather than clinical. The preferred atmosphere is autumnal, natural, soft, and premium, with restraint so fasting data remains clear.

## Anti-references

The app should not feel clinical, gamified, overly colorful, competitive, noisy, or like a social feed.

Avoid medical-dashboard aesthetics, streak-driven fitness energy, achievement systems, loud progress theatrics, and interfaces where decoration competes with fasting status. Warmth should not become generic beige wellness branding; it should feel intentional, natural, and premium.

## Design Principles

1. Make privacy legible.
   Users should always understand whether they are tracking personally or sharing current activity with a Fasting Circle.

2. Keep fasting status effortless to read.
   Current state, elapsed time, remaining time, and target end time should be visually immediate without requiring interpretation.

3. Support without pressure.
   The app should encourage and reassure, not shame, rank, compare, or over-celebrate.

4. Use warmth as intimacy, not decoration.
   Rounded corners, autumnal natural colors, and smooth motion should make the app feel composed and humane while preserving clarity.

5. Prefer familiar mobile affordances.
   Controls should feel native and predictable on iOS and Android, with motion used for state change and feedback rather than spectacle.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Do not rely on color alone to communicate fasting state or completion. Smooth animations should respect reduced-motion settings with simpler transitions. Touch targets should be comfortable for one-handed mobile use.
